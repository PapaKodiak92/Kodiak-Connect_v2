# Kodiak Connect Privacy Policy Draft

Status: Working draft. Not final. Requires legal review before public launch.

Effective date: TBD

Kodiak Connect is operated by Kodiak Holdings LLC.

This draft is intended to guide product development and policy review. It should not be published as the final Privacy Policy until reviewed and approved.

## 1. Overview

Kodiak Connect is a communication platform designed for individuals, families, and businesses. The service may include accounts, servers/spaces, channels, direct messages, family account tools, business tools, safety reporting, and Trust & Safety review.

We aim to collect only the information needed to operate, secure, improve, and protect Kodiak Connect.

## 2. Information we may collect

### Account information

- Username.
- Email address.
- Password authentication data handled through the authentication system.
- Account type or relationship status, such as individual, parent/guardian, child/dependent, business owner, or staff.
- Profile information users choose to provide.

### Communication information

Depending on the type of space and its privacy settings, Kodiak Connect may process:

- Servers/spaces a user joins or creates.
- Channel membership.
- Message metadata.
- Message content in safety-monitored or non-end-to-end encrypted spaces.
- Attachments or media shared through the service.
- Reports and moderation actions.

If end-to-end encrypted features are offered, message content in those areas may not be readable by Kodiak Connect servers. The product must clearly identify which spaces are safety-monitored and which spaces are encrypted/private.

### Family account information

For family accounts, we may collect:

- Parent/guardian account information.
- Linked child/dependent account information.
- Family invitations.
- Restrictions and parental control settings.
- Family safety reports according to policy.

### Business account information

For business accounts, we may collect:

- Business workspace information.
- Business owner/admin information.
- Member lists.
- Channels and roles.
- Billing or subscription information if paid plans are offered.

### Safety and security information

We may collect or generate:

- User reports.
- Red-flag safety signals.
- Incident records.
- Trust & Safety reviewer actions.
- Enforcement history.
- Audit logs.
- Device/session information.
- IP address and basic technical logs.

## 3. How we use information

We may use information to:

- Provide Kodiak Connect features.
- Authenticate users.
- Deliver messages and notifications.
- Manage servers/spaces and channels.
- Support family account controls.
- Support business workspaces.
- Detect, prevent, and respond to abuse, fraud, threats, exploitation, harassment, malware, and policy violations.
- Review reports and red-flag incidents.
- Enforce Terms of Service and Community Guidelines.
- Maintain security and reliability.
- Respond to support requests.
- Comply with legal obligations.

## 4. Trust and Safety review

Kodiak Connect may review reports, red-flag incidents, and related context to protect users and enforce platform rules.

Server owners, business owners, parents/guardians, moderators, and premium users do not have immunity from platform safety review.

Trust & Safety access should be limited to authorized staff and logged.

## 5. Family and child/dependent accounts

Family account features should be designed with extra privacy protections.

Before launch, Kodiak Connect must define:

- Parental consent requirements.
- Child/dependent account setup.
- Parent/guardian access limits.
- Child/dependent notices.
- Data retention and deletion rights.
- Safety reporting and escalation workflows.

Kodiak Connect should not launch child/dependent account features until these requirements are complete and legally reviewed.

## 6. Sharing information

We may share information:

- With users as needed to provide the service, such as messages in shared spaces.
- With parent/guardian accounts according to family account policy.
- With business workspace administrators according to business policy.
- With service providers that help operate Kodiak Connect.
- With Trust & Safety staff for safety review.
- When required by law.
- In emergencies involving risk of harm.
- To report legally required safety incidents through the proper process.

We should not sell personal information.

## 7. Data retention

Kodiak Connect should retain information only as long as needed for service operation, safety, legal compliance, dispute resolution, and security.

Specific retention periods must be defined before public launch for:

- Account information.
- Messages.
- Deleted messages.
- Safety reports.
- Red-flag incidents.
- Audit logs.
- Billing records.
- Support records.

## 8. User choices and controls

Depending on account type and legal requirements, users may be able to:

- Access account information.
- Update account information.
- Delete an account.
- Leave servers/spaces.
- Report users or content.
- Block users.
- Manage family account settings.
- Request data export or deletion where applicable.

## 9. Security

Kodiak Connect should use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect information.

Security requirements include:

- HTTPS.
- Access controls.
- Audit logs.
- Backup and restore procedures.
- Least-privilege staff access.
- Secure secret handling.
- Incident response process.

## 10. Legal requests and emergency disclosures

Kodiak Connect should have a documented process for responding to legal requests, emergency requests, and legally required reports.

Staff should not improvise legal responses without following the documented process.

## 11. Changes to this policy

Kodiak Connect may update this Privacy Policy. Users should be notified of material changes according to policy.
