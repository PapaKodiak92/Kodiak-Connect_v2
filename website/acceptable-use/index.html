Acceptable Use Policy

Effective Date: June 4, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy explains what is not allowed on Kodiak Connect.

Contact: support@kodiak-connect.com

1. General Rule

Use Kodiak Connect responsibly. Do not use the service to harm people, violate law, attack systems, exploit minors, or disrupt the service.

2. Prohibited Conduct

You may not use Kodiak Connect to:

  • Harass, threaten, bully, stalk, or abuse others.
  • Promote or coordinate violence or credible threats.
  • Exploit, sexualize, endanger, groom, or harm minors.
  • Share intimate images without consent.
  • Impersonate another person, company, moderator, admin, or Kodiak Connect representative.
  • Spam, mass-message, phish, scam, or manipulate users.
  • Distribute malware, credential stealers, viruses, or harmful code.
  • Attempt unauthorized access to accounts, servers, systems, or data.
  • Evade bans, restrictions, rate limits, or moderation controls.
  • Share illegal content or use Kodiak Connect to plan illegal activity.
  • Violate intellectual property rights.
  • Scrape, harvest, or collect user information without permission.
  • Misuse reporting, moderation, family, business, or admin tools.
  • Interfere with service performance, stability, or security.

3. Content Restrictions

Users may not share content that is illegal, exploitative, abusive, malicious, or designed to harm others.

Kodiak Connect may remove content or restrict accounts when we believe content violates this policy or creates risk.

4. Child Safety

Content or behavior involving child exploitation, grooming, sexualization of minors, or attempts to contact minors for harmful purposes is strictly prohibited and may be reported to appropriate authorities.

5. Security Research

Do not test, scan, attack, reverse engineer, or probe Kodiak Connect systems without written permission. If you discover a security issue, contact support@kodiak-connect.com.

6. Enforcement

Kodiak Connect may warn users, remove content, restrict features, suspend accounts, terminate accounts, preserve evidence, or report activity when appropriate.
